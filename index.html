Destruction of the Amazon rainforest

The Amazon biome encompasses 1.7 billion acres of land which is shared among eight countries in South America (Brazil, Peru, Columbia, Venezuela, Ecuador, Bolivia, Guyana, Suriname) and few parts of French Guiana territory.An estimate of 60% of the Amazon Rainforest is within Brazil, 13% of the rainforest is in Peru and tiny parts in remaining countries.The Amazon,is the lung of our planet, which produced 20% of the world's oxygen, is on fire. According to National Geographic, around 17 percent of the Amazon rainforest has been destroyed over the past 50 years due to deforestation.

Why there is destruction?

Desforestation was the primary way to maintain the livelihood for the farmers, who cut down trees to produce crops for their families and local consumption.Vast areas of rainforest were cut-down for cattle pasture and soy farms, drowned for dams, dug up for minerals, and bulldozed for towns and colonization projects.Large blocks of rainforest have been granted for exploration and exploitation licenses in recent years.Deforestation leads to a large number of observed fires across the Amazon during the dry season. It is estimated that 99% of the wildfires in the Amazon basin are a result of human actions, either on purpose or accidentally.Cattle ranching is the leading cause of deforestation in the Amazon rainforest.Most of the beef is destined for urban markets, whereas leather and other cattle products are primarily for export markets.The UK imported Â£1bn-worth of Brazilian beef over five years from three companies linked to major annual deforestation in the Amazon.

The deforestation and forest fires in the amazon lead to the acceleration of carbon-di-oxide emissions.A huge amount of carbon-di-oxide is emitted from the countries covered by the Amazon forest, there by increasing the global emission levels.

What happens to the world when the Amazon is destructed?

Due to deforestation and urbanization, scientists estimate that we are near the tipping point where the Amazon can no longer function as a carbon sink.The rainforest contains 90-140 billion metric tonnes of carbon.The Amazon basin is the largest rainforest in the world, and a vital carbon store that slows down global warming.The air quality will drop and we will start inhaling more Carbon-di-oxide.Trees and plants absorb carbon dioxide, and as they perish from the Amazon rainforest, they will release billions of tons of carbon that the rainforest has been storing for years. There will be warmer temperatures, long droughts, and frequent floods which will take a toll on agriculture, water, and food supplies.
